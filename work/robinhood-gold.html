Robinhood

Growing Robinhood Gold

Robinhood Gold is a $5/month membership offering interest on uninvested cash and advanced investing tools. I designed and tested growth experiments that substantially increased Gold subscribers and deposits.

Role Content Design
Team Product, Product Design, Data Science, Engineering

Problem

Gold is Robinhood’s paid premium membership. For $5 a month, customers can earn interest on uninvested cash, access premium research and data, and utilize other investing-related benefits. Historically, Robinhood has struggled to effectively market and grow Robinhood Gold in its product surfaces.

Strategy

I aligned on these core principles with my product design partner:

  1. Focus on gen-pop value prop: Emphasize competitive interest rates on uninvested cash as the primary appeal of Gold.
  2. Meet people in context: Integrate upsells naturally into core user journeys, like funding their account.
  3. Make value tangible: Use earnings projections to help users understand concrete benefits.

Process

I led a workshop establishing a growth design process playbook with structured steps: hypothesis definition, experiment design, stakeholder management, and documentation and socialization.

The circular process emphasizes that analysis directly influences subsequent prioritization. Timeline: 2-3 weeks from conception to implementation.

Experiment 1: Gold native funding upsell

Hypothesis: Integrating the Gold upsell within the funding step rather than after would increase adoption by contextualizing deposit benefits at deposit time.

Variants tested

  • Variant 1: Repurposed selector design, post-deposit positioning
  • Variant 2: Moved upsell pre-deposit with contextual messaging about upcoming deposit
Screen 1 of 3

Variant 1

Repurposed selector design, post-deposit positioning

Outcomes

+110%
increase in Gold adoption (statistically significant). Neutral churn rates compared to other upsell channels. Directional increase in first-time deposit amounts.

Experiment 2: Gold earnings calculator for Gold members

Objective: Reinforce new members' decision and encourage larger initial deposits.

Hypothesis: Displaying potential earnings during the deposit step would result in larger deposits.

Design

Contextual banner below deposit input showing combined potential earnings from interest + deposit boost, with additional disclosure below.

Outcomes

+3% to +7%
net deposit increase for users with $1K+ linked (statistically significant)
+5% to +10%
net deposit increase for users with $2K+ linked. Results replicated after feature deprecation.

Experiment 3: Gold earnings calculator for non-Gold members

Hypothesis: Surfacing earnings projections post-transfer would drive Gold adoption among existing users making substantial deposits.

Design strategy

  • Contextual bottom sheet appearing after transfer completion
  • Audience segmentation: shown only to users depositing at/above "breakeven point" (~$1,100)
  • Content focuses on Gold "paying for itself" through earned interest
  • CTAs: "Earn with Gold" (primary) and "Continue without Gold"

Key design considerations

Legal & Compliance Challenges: Extensive negotiation required for earnings projections. Required hedging language: "could earn," "may make sense," "potentially." Disclosure management balanced promissory concerns with user clarity.

Content Testing: Unmoderated surveys tested "pays for itself" framing. Found excessive cognitive load; simplified messaging tested instead. Value-based CTAs consistently outperformed.

Outcomes

+3.49%
in Gold adoption (statistically significant)
+0.91%
in incremental net deposits (directional, not significant)

Key learnings

  • Defining your hypothesis at the outset is important — you can use it as an anchor throughout the project
  • Growth design requires strategy development alongside iterative testing
  • Compliance integration as a design constraint requiring creative solutions
  • Audience segmentation for relevance
  • Value-forward messaging and CTAs consistently outperform
  • Context-native placements over disruptive overlays
